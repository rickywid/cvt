AutoCAD Final Project (1st semester)

  • Course: CAD Fundamentals (BEA101)
    •

This final project required us to demonstrate the skills we developed throughout the semester by creating a detailed 2D drawing based on a fictional site plan while meeting specific project requirements. Through this assignment, I reinforced my understanding of AutoCAD fundamentals by accurately producing a complete drawing from start to finish.

The project emphasized essential skills such as creating and managing layers, layer styles, blocks, annotations, labels, dimensions, hatching, UCS/WCS and more. Additionally, I gained practical experience working in both model space and paper space to prepare and export the final DWG file as a PDF and other alternative file formats.

Skills learned:

  • layers
  • layer styles
  • blocks
  • annotations
  • labels
  • dimensions
  • hatching
  • UCS/WCS

AutoCAD Subdivision Assignment (2nd semester)

  • Course: CAD Drafting II (CRX342)
    •

For this assignment we create a subdivision plan with profile views that are essential in subdivision planning as they provide detailed snapshots of the land, showing both existing and proposed conditions along with utilities like storm and sanitary systems. They help understand the terrainâ€™s highs and lows, guiding effective road and site design. These drawings ensure that infrastructure such as manholes and pipes align correctly with the ground, preventing construction issues. Additionally, accurate profile views demonstrate compliance with regulations and facilitate approval by clearly presenting the planned project.

During this project, students learned about subdivision planning and drawing proposed and existing ground profiles. Students also learned how to design and layout storm and sanitary pipes according to varying ground elevations.

Skills learned:

  • subdivision planning
  • storm design
  • pipe design
  • ground elevation profiles
